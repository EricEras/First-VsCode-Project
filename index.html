Leatherback Sea Turtles

The leatherback sea turtle is the world's largest turtle and lacks scales and a hard shell. They are highly migratory and accomplished divers, with the deepest recorded dive reaching nearly 4,000 feet. Leatherbacks face threats such as incidental capture in fishing gear, hunting, and egg collection. They are rapidly declining, especially in the Pacific, where recovery efforts are being made to prevent extinction. NOAA Fisheries and partners are working to study, protect, and recover leatherbacks through regulations, recovery plans, research, monitoring, and conservation projects.
