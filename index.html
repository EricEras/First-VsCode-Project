Interesting Sea Turtle Facts

  1. All sea turtle species are endangered or threatened due to human activities such as fishing, pollution, and development.
  2. Sea turtles can travel long distances, with some species traveling over 1,000 miles from their nesting site to their feeding grounds.
  3. Sea turtles lay their eggs in a nest they dig in the sand with their rear flippers. The group of eggs is called a clutch.
  4. There are seven species of sea turtles: green, hawksbill, loggerhead, Kemp's ridley, olive ridley, flatback, and leatherback.
  5. Sea turtles can hold their breath for several hours while diving.

Leatherback Sea Turtles

The leatherback sea turtle is the world's largest turtle and lacks scales and a hard shell. They are highly migratory and accomplished divers, with the deepest recorded dive reaching nearly 4,000 feet. Leatherbacks face threats such as incidental capture in fishing gear, hunting, and egg collection. They are rapidly declining, especially in the Pacific, where recovery efforts are being made to prevent extinction.
